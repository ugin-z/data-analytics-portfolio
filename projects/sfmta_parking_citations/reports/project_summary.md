# Revenue Analysis Report  
San Francisco Parking Citations (2021–2025)

---

## Executive Summary

Parking citation revenue in 2025 is significantly outperforming historical trends. Revenue is also highly concentrated across a small number of violation types, indicating structural dependency on limited categories.

---

## 2025 Revenue Performance

2025 year-to-date revenue reached $174M, representing a +55% increase compared to the 2021–2024 historical average.
Revenue acceleration is most visible in Q3-Q4, suggesting increased enforcement activity or improved fine collection during the second half of the year.
This indicates strong short-term performance relative to prior years.

---

## Revenue Concentration

Revenue distribution follows a strong Pareto pattern:

- Top 5 violation types generate ~66% of total revenue.
- Top 10 violation types generate ~78%.
- A single violation category (Street Cleaning) accounts for nearly 40% of total revenue.

This level of concentration suggests revenue sensitivity to changes in enforcement or compliance within a limited set of categories.

---

## Structural Implications

- Revenue is not broadly diversified.
- Policy or operational changes affecting top violation types could materially impact total revenue.
- Monitoring concentration trends over time is recommended.
